Crimson

"Scout! look out behind you!"

Scout turned, but saw nothing, for he had already been struck in the head by the falling log. Thunder ran over to see if his friend was okay.

"Scout? Scout!"

He wasn't moving, wasn't responding. It looked as if he had eaten dirt, literally.

"Man, this isn't funny. If you're messing with me please stop, man. Scout!"

Scout had never taken a joke this far; Thunder guessed he wasn't joking. What to do? Should I try to move him? He's alive, right? Yes; he's breathing but softly. He just didn't seem to be doing anything else.

Thunder wondered what was the best course of action in this situation. What did aliens need to recover from logs to the head? Perhaps this was just a temporary condition; Scout would recover on his own. Man, why hadn't we talked about this before? Thunder thought to himself.

He decided to clean Scout's face a bit; there was dirt smashed into his mouth; some blood was coming from somewhere; it was only a couple CCs and didn't seem to be gushing. Thunder splashed water from his canteen onto his towel and swept away the larger bits of dirt. He dabbed around Scout's lips to clean up the blood.

Scout moaned a bit. A sort of pained noise, but nonetheless a joy for Thunder to hear.

"time is it?"

"Scout? It's 3 o'clock. Are you okay?"

"3."

Thunder, relieved, wiped a bit less cautiously; his friend seemed to be recovering.

"wa ter?"

"Here." Thunder drizzled a bit from his canteen into Scout's mouth.

Scout swallowed a small amount. "What time, is it?"

"It's still 3 o'clock."

"mm. what happened?"

"Heh. Um, our little experiment didn't work as planned. The log came swinging down before you were ready and schnockered you pretty hard. You were out cold for a few minutes."

"experiment. Is it 5 o'clock?"

"No; it's 3 o'clock. Three oh six now. Why? What happens at 5?"

"have to go home."

"Really?"

Scout got a far away look in his eyes that Thunder hadn't previously seen. This was a bit different than a distracted-in-thought look or simple daydreaming. This was more like Scout's entire Self had left his body in search of something; maybe it had been knocked free by the log. Maybe it was nothing; Thunder didn't worry about it.

"Here. Let's get you cleaned up."

Thunder got up and held out his paw to help Scout up. Scout obliged, but didn't seem fully aware. He got up carefully, but with minimal difficulty and followed Thunder toward the river.

The river was a bit large to be called a creek, but really it wasn't large enough to be a river. On some days it hardly flowed enough to be called running water, but generally they could use it to splash around in the naturally formed hole at the base of the falls. This day the water ran heavily enough to be heard from a fair distance; Thunder knew by its sound the water would be deep and refreshing.

He walked ahead to the pool and suddenly thought to turn around before he got in. Scout wasn't with him.

Thunder retraced his steps and found Scout laying in a sort of fetal position, completely unconscious, completely unresponsive. Some hint of a movie came back to Thunder's mind, must be kept awake or something something coma.

"Oh no. Scout! Scout! Wake up, Scout! You have to wake up!" Thunder pushed Scout over onto his back, but he still didn't respond. "Scout! Scout, come on man, wake up! You have to wake up, Scout!"

Thunder ran to the river and looked for some way to carry water back. Why had he left his canteen back at the log?

Every idea crossed Thunder's brain at once in a traffic jam. Splashing the water back to Scout, carrying some in a leaf, carrying some in his paws. Ah; his fur. Thunder lept into the pool then back out again as quickly as he could and ran back to Scout and stood over him and shook his body in his best soggy shaggy dog impression. He wringed his ear over Scout's face. That did it. Scout sputtered at the spattering of water and coughed a bit.

"Scout! Scout!!" Thunder wringed the water from his other ear onto Scout's face. "Scout! Wake Up Scout!"

"what time is it?"

Oh thank God. "Scout! It's Five O Clock! You're late! Wake up!!"

"It's 5?"

"Yes, Wake up! You have to wake up now!"

Scout began to get up.

"Quick Scout! Come with me."

Thunder took him by the hand this time and led Scout as quickly as he thought Scout could walk in his dazed state. At the river, Thunder decided not to push Scout in, but jumped in again himself. He thrashed as much water as he could up at Scout.

"Hey. Thunder. What are you doing?"

"You've got to stay *awake*! I'm waking you up!"

"I am awake."

"Get into the water. Carefully." Thunder wondered at the horrible ironic possibility of Scout dashing his head on the rocks diving into the water. What a nightmare that would be. "Can you swim? Er, are you okay to swim right now?"

"I'm okay now."

"Good; get in."

"No; I'll be alright. Really."

Thunder sighed, "okay." He nodded after a pause. "Okay. I'll trust that you are okay." He splashed his friend again.

"Hey!"

"Just making sure, buddy."
